# Dixon City Council Meeting Summary — April 21, 2026

## Police Officers Honored for Saving a Life

The meeting opened with Police Chief Ryan Bivins presenting Lifesaving Awards to Sergeant Dietz, Officer DeHaan, and Officer White. On March 17, the three responded to a welfare check and found an unresponsive person in a running vehicle inside a closed garage. Sergeant Dietz broke out garage windows to ventilate the area while officers forced entry, pulled the individual from the vehicle, and performed chest compressions until a pulse returned. The person was transported by Dixon City Fire, spent several days in the hospital, and made a full recovery. Chief Bivins noted that sensitive details were omitted out of respect for those involved.

## Young Dixonian Proposes Bridge Name

Spencer Thompson, 18, joined the meeting via Zoom from college roughly a thousand miles away to propose a name for the pedestrian bridge currently known as Project Rock. Thompson suggested "Petunia Crossing," arguing the name honors Dixon's longstanding tradition of petunia plantings along South Galena Avenue, which dates to 1960. He cautioned against a generic or overly broad name, pointing to the Veterans Memorial Bridge on Galena Avenue as an example of a formal name that fell out of public use. Thompson encouraged the council to open naming to the public. Councilman Mike Venier called the proposal a good springboard for a broader naming campaign.

## Economic Development Update from LCIDA

Tom Demmer of the Lee County Industrial Development Association delivered his annual update. Key points:

- **Existing business support:** Companies including Rainer Garage Doors, Bunnell Industries, Sewer Equipment Company, and RP Lumber have continued to invest locally. LCIDA and Discover Dixon organized a manufacturing hiring fair on short notice after Wahl Clipper announced layoffs in Sterling, drawing dozens of job seekers.
- **Power capacity:** ComEd is engineering an additional feeder line to the Dixon Industrial Park to support advanced manufacturing's growing electricity demands.
- **Solar energy:** Two large solar projects in Lee County — South Dixon Solar and the even larger Stewart Creek Solar — could pair local renewable generation with power-hungry users scouting sites.
- **Demolition grants:** The city and Lee County used Strong Communities Grant funds to demolish blighted homes, including three LCIDA-owned houses on West First Street in the Viaduct Point redevelopment area. Those lots are now cleared and ready for commercial and residential development.
- **Opportunity Zones 2.0:** Two Dixon-area census tracts — southwest Dixon and a southeastern tract stretching to the tollway and the 65-acre city-owned parcel behind Walmart — are the only tracts in Lee County eligible for new federal Opportunity Zone designation. Nominations are due April 30, with decisions expected by July 1. The program offers enhanced tax benefits steered toward rural communities.

## FY 2026–27 Budget Approved

The council unanimously approved the city's fiscal year 2026–27 budget. Finance Director Becky Leslie outlined changes since the budget was placed on file a month ago: a $3,000 increase for council professional development (sister cities), a small salary market adjustment in administration, $3,500 added for HR software, $25,000 added to the Main Street contract, $400,000 in grant revenue for new radios, roughly $350,000 rolled over for the fuel island project, and $85,000 for a marked squad car and $140,000 for unmarked squads. City Manager Danny Langloss praised Leslie, who is approaching retirement, for building a transparent and responsive budget process.

## Gateway Subdivision Replat

The council approved a replat of lot 12 in the Dixon Gateway Subdivision, splitting the 4.1-acre YMCA lot into two parcels — 2.83 acres for the YMCA and a new 1.27-acre developable lot to the north. The Plan Commission recommended approval.

## Gaming Parlor Liquor License Reduced

The council voted to reduce the number of Class I and I-1 liquor licenses — which cover standalone gaming parlors required to serve at least beer and wine — from three to two. The reduction follows Stonewood's decision not to renew its license, which expires April 30. City Manager Langloss told the council that leaving the license open would likely attract 25 to 50 applicants, and reminded members that the council's stated direction has been to discourage standalone slot parlor operations. The council approved the reduction unanimously.

## Budget Amendments

Four internal budget amendments passed unanimously:

- **Library:** Shifted funds from full-time salaries to part-time wages and library materials (no new money).
- **Municipal Band:** Moved $2,350 from unused operating and travel lines to cover the spring concert and St. Patrick's Day parade.
- **Police:** Transferred $8,500 from the social worker medical insurance line to uniforms and small tools/equipment.
- **Water Department:** Added $25,000 for rising chemical costs (fluoride, phosphate, etc.) and $5,000 for information technology upgrades.

## Airport Runway Engineering Agreement Updated

The council approved an updated construction engineering agreement with Crawford, Murphy & Tilly for rehabilitation of Runway 12-30 at the Dixon Airport. Delays between IDOT's Division of Aeronautics and the contractor pushed the not-to-exceed price from $107,400 to $115,900. Funding remains 90% federal, 5% state, and 5% city.

## Seven Brew Coffee Coming; Two Redevelopment Agreements Approved

The council referred a special use petition from Brew Horizons LLC (the corporate entity behind Seven Brew drive-through coffee) to the Plan Commission for a location at 1617 South Galena Avenue, next to the new Tropical Smoothie.

The council also approved two 10-year TIF redevelopment agreements with Dixon Gateway Plaza 2 LLC (the developer, Dixon Land Group):

1. **1611 South Galena Avenue** — a multi-tenant building housing Tropical Smoothie, Great Clips, OSF urgent care, and future tenants.
2. **1617 South Galena Avenue** — the standalone Seven Brew location.

Both agreements provide a 70% property tax rebate for the first five years and 50% for the remaining five.

## Water Department Report

Cameron Magni, stepping into the water department manager role, reported that the crew has installed approximately 1,400 feet of new water main on the west end this season, with 65 new water services to be replaced in-house — a continued cost savings. Only nine water main breaks occurred over winter, an improvement attributed to the city's ongoing replacement of aging mains. The department's other major project is recoating Water Tower 9 near Culver's; a contract award is expected at the next meeting, pending coordination with Verizon antennas on the structure.

## Council Notes

- Councilman Chris Bishop promoted the America 250 event calendar now live on DiscoverDixon.com, including a June 27 ball at the Loveland Community Center benefiting the Wounded Warrior Project, featuring the Yorkville Big Band and the Time Bandits.
- Councilman Dennis Considine shared that a visit to a church celebration in a neighboring community reinforced that people travel to Dixon specifically for petunias, restaurants, and shops.
- Councilwoman Mary Oros highlighted the cooperative spirit among downtown boutiques, which organized a joint event welcoming the reopened J's Boutique.
- Councilman Mike Venier noted the police department's newly released 59-page annual report, now available to the public, and flagged pending state legislation on e-bikes and "micromobility devices" that Chief Bivins and City Attorney Robert LeSage have been tracking.
- Public Works Director Matt Heckman was recognized as Dixon's Citizen of the Year at Friday's Chamber/Main Street gala.

The meeting adjourned after all agenda items were completed.
