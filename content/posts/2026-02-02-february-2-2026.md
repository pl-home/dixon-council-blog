# Dixon City Council Meeting Summary — February 3, 2026

## Police Officer Honored for Saving a Life

The meeting opened with the highlight of the evening: Officer Colin Grady received a commendation for saving a man's life on January 8th. Grady was first on scene at a single-vehicle crash where a car struck a house, severing a gas line. He pulled the injured driver from the wreckage and applied a tourniquet — his first time doing so outside of training. Dr. Brand, an ER physician at KSB Hospital, told the council that the driver, who was on blood thinners due to vascular disease, would have died without Grady's quick action. "If it wasn't for you, that patient would definitely have expired," Brand said.

## Three New Members Sworn into Police Department

Two new officers and a new deputy chief took their oaths:

- **Officer Griffey Rodríguez**, a Dixon native and Dixon High School graduate who earned his bachelor's degree in sociology and criminology from NIU. He graduated from the Sauk Valley Police Academy in December and is currently in field training.

- **Officer Christian Yepsen**, from Princeton, Illinois, also a December graduate of the Sauk Valley Police Academy. He holds an associate's degree in criminal justice from Illinois Valley Community College and is in field training.

- **Deputy Chief Christopher Scott**, promoted after eight-plus years with Dixon PD and 17 years total in law enforcement. Scott has served in multiple roles and has been a driving force behind community programs including Shop with a Cop, Clothes for Kids, and a scholarship program for graduating high school seniors that has supported over 700 children. In brief remarks, Scott told his fellow officers: "This department succeeds or fails together, and we will not fail."

## Streets & Properties Update

Councilman Tyler Veneer presented a department overview covering 2025 accomplishments and upcoming work:

- The **Oakwood maintenance building** is complete and operational, giving staff a heated, safe workspace.
- A **brine system** is now in use for winter road treatment, helping roads return to bare pavement faster.
- **150 tons of asphalt** were laid for road repairs, and nearly **2,440 linear feet of sidewalk** were replaced — roughly eight city blocks — half through the city's resident sidewalk program, which reopens for signups in April. Doing the CIP sidewalk work in-house saved the city an estimated **$106,000**.
- Current winter projects include **rehabilitating the River Street parking lot railing**, installing **16 new street lights on Galena Avenue** (wired and ready when weather allows), and rebuilding the **dirt shed** in-house for approximately **$60,000 in savings**.
- This season, the department plans a push on **crack sealing** to extend road life, along with continued sidewalk replacement prioritized around schools, bus stops, and parks.

## Fire Department Update

Chief Buskohl reported the department logged **2,700 calls for service** in 2025 (2,170 EMS, 530 fire/rescue) and over **7,000 training hours**.

Key developments include:

- A **Stop the Bleed program** that placed individual trauma kits in every classroom in Dixon schools, funded through collaboration among the fire department, Dixon Rural, Dixon PD charitable associations, and the Legends of the Rock fishing tournament. Training for school staff is nearly complete.
- A **mass notification system called HyperReach** that allows police, fire, and public works to send alerts to residents — for emergencies, road closures, gas leaks, and more. The system covers both the city of Dixon and the Dixon Rural fire protection area. An advanced feature allowing geographically targeted emergency alerts to cell phones is still in development.
- A **new first-grade fire safety curriculum and hazard house**, replacing outdated equipment, launching this month.
- Continued investment in professional development: multiple firefighters completed degrees, certifications, and paramedic licensures in 2025. The department now has 17 swift water technicians, six divers, five technical rescue technicians, four licensed drone pilots, and three tactical medics.
- A **firefighter apprentice program** designed to engage recruits between high school graduation and age 21. Two apprentices are currently enrolled, with room for one more. Interested parties should contact the fire station.
- A new **hiring testing consortium** with Sauk Valley College and five other departments streamlines the written exam process for firefighter candidates.

## Routine Business

- **Minutes** from the January 20 meeting were approved.
- **Total claims of $2,787,924.71** were approved. The mayor broke down the larger items: $600,000 to a CD investment; $116,441 and $484,180 for wastewater treatment plant engineering and improvements; $362,428 and $748,867 for fire and police pension payments (previously approved); and $233,967 for the city fuel station.
- An **ordinance** was approved to dispose of outdated city computing equipment.
- A **budget amendment** moved $3,000 within the IT budget to purchase an unplanned computer.
- A **police department budget amendment** shifted $18,000 from part-time wages to overtime and $4,000 from the crisis coordinator budget to uniforms — no new spending.
- A **resolution** approved the temporary closure of the eastbound outside lane of Illinois Route 2 on the morning of July 4 for the Reagan 5K run/walk.
- An **engineering agreement** with Crawford, Murphy & Tilly for up to $54,000 was approved for construction-phase services to replace the **AWOS (Automated Weather Observing System)** at the city airport. The project is 90% federally funded, 5% state, and 5% city.

## Council Comments

- The mayor recognized retired Deputy Chief Doug Lehman for 30 years in law enforcement, including 26-plus with Dixon PD.
- Councilman Considine praised the fire department based on a recent personal experience, calling Dixon's public safety departments "one of the finest in the state."
- Councilman Veneer noted that **February is Black History Month** and announced that two recipients of the **Ike Mercer Certificate of Achievement Award** will be recognized at the next meeting. He also promoted a **trivia night fundraiser on February 21 at the Elks Lodge** benefiting the Al Morrison Memorial Baseball Program, now in its sixth year, serving over 400 families.

The council then moved into executive session to review closed session minutes.
