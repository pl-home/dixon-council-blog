# Dixon City Council Meeting Summary — May 4, 2026

## The Basics

The Dixon City Council met Monday evening with all members present: Mayor Glen Hughes, and Councilmen Mike Venier, Chris Bishop, Dennis Considine, and Mary Oros. The council approved $1,177,514.81 in labor and outside claims and moved through a mix of financial, personnel, and infrastructure business.

---

## Finance Director Gives Final Report

Becky Leslie delivered what she described as likely her last report to the council. Heather, her successor, will be sworn in as finance director on June 1. Leslie said she feels confident the transition has been handled well, thanks to what she called a genuine succession plan that gave both women time to work through once-a-year duties like the audit and budget together.

On the financial front, Leslie reported encouraging preliminary numbers for the fiscal year ending April 30. Sales tax revenue grew 15% — a welcome surprise after the city had budgeted conservatively. She also noted motel tax collections have climbed significantly with two new hotels in operation, rising from roughly $75,000–$80,000 annually to $140,000 since January 2024. The annual audit process is now underway.

---

## Water Tower Painting Contract Tabled After Union Objection

The most contentious item of the evening involved the proposed awarding of a painting contract for the Well No. 9 elevated water storage tank to a firm called LC United.

David Headley, a business agent for Painters District Council 30, spoke during public comment urging the council to reject or delay the award. He cited what he described as a troubling safety record — including a worker fall from a water tower that resulted in life-altering injuries and more than 40 OSHA violations — along with prevailing wage act violations and ongoing state investigations. Headley argued that the lowest bid doesn't necessarily mean the lowest cost when corners are cut.

City Attorney Robert LeSage told the council that supporting documentation from the union had been provided just minutes before the meeting. He recommended tabling the item to give staff time to review the new information. LeSage acknowledged the timing was awkward and noted the documentation wasn't from a publicly accessible database — it likely required a FOIA request or union contacts at the Department of Labor to obtain.

The council voted unanimously to table the matter.

---

## New Police Tahoe Approved

The council approved the purchase of a 2026 Chevrolet Tahoe police package from Carl Emergency Vehicles for $84,274.89. The vehicle replaces a 2013 Dodge Charger, which will be traded in for $2,500. Deputy Chief Scott explained that virtually no equipment from the old vehicle can be transferred except the camera system, internet system, and printer, necessitating a full equipment outfitting at approximately $29,500.

---

## Budget Amendments

Two budget resolutions passed unanimously. One addressed fire department overtime spending; the other shifted $16,000 within the police budget to cover overtime expenses and $3,500 for professional development. Both involved moving money between existing line items — no new appropriations.

---

## Tim Shipman Named Assistant City Manager

With Public Works Director Matt Heckman retiring June 5, the council approved the appointment of Building Official Tim Shipman as the new Assistant City Manager. City Manager Danny Langloss explained this is a stipend position added on top of a department head's existing duties — not a standalone role. A selection committee that included Mayor Hughes, Councilman Bishop, and others made the recommendation.

Langloss praised Heckman's eight-and-a-half years in the role and spoke highly of both candidates who applied. Mayor Hughes echoed that sentiment. Councilman Considine abstained from the vote; the motion passed otherwise.

---

## Board Appointments

- **Brad** (last name not specified) was reappointed to the Municipal Band Board for a term ending April 30, 2028. Mayor Hughes noted he has received positive feedback about Brad's service.
- **Jennifer Sarno** was reappointed to the Dixon Historic Theater Board through March 2029.
- **Rebecca Demmer** was appointed to the Dixon Historic Theater Board, filling the seat vacated by Councilman Venier, who stepped down from the board but remains actively involved with the theater. Mayor Hughes thanked Venier for his service.

---

## Subdivision Plat Referred

A final plat for the re-subdivision of Lots 2A and 2B of the Dixon Gateway Subdivision was referred to the Plan Commission for review — a routine procedural step with no council action required at this time.

---

## Other Notes

Councilman Venier presented Mayor Hughes with an "America 250" hat commemorating the nation's upcoming 250th birthday (1776–2026). Hughes said he'd be wearing it around town. Brief remarks were also made praising Councilman Bishop's leadership on what appears to be related America 250 planning efforts.

The meeting was adjourned.
