# Dixon City Council Meeting Summary — June 1, 2026

## Staff Changes Take Center Stage

The June 1 meeting was dominated by farewells and new appointments as the city turns a page in its leadership.

**Heather McCarter** was unanimously appointed as the city's new treasurer, succeeding retiring Finance Director Becky Leslie. City Manager Danny Langloss Jr. described McCarter's path from her 2020 hiring through a deliberate succession plan, noting that Leslie herself identified McCarter early on as a potential successor. McCarter was sworn in during the meeting.

**Tim Shipman**, a 33-year city employee who has served as firefighter, lieutenant, fire chief, and building official, was sworn in as assistant city manager.

**Matt Huyett** was promoted to public works director, replacing the retiring **Matt Heckman**. Cameron Magney was named water manager and Mike Bryska as general foreman.

Both Heckman and Leslie received extensive praise from the council and staff. Langloss credited Heckman's work over eight-plus years on the Gateway development, the current bridge project, and repaving more than 40 percent of city roads. Leslie will stay on part-time to assist with human resources and system implementation.

McCarter offered brief remarks of her own, thanking colleagues for their patience and mentorship during her transition.

## Ordinances and Resolutions

**Class D Liquor Licenses:** The council approved reducing Class D liquor licenses to 16 after Shinney's changed its licensing structure, freeing up an unused license.

**Property Donation:** The council accepted donation of a vacant multi-unit building at 117 East 4th Street. The city plans to demolish the structure and prepare the site for redevelopment using grant funds.

**Gateway Subdivision Replat:** Dixon Land Group LLC received approval to adjust lot lines in the Gateway subdivision. A lot designated for a coffee shop was enlarged from 0.43 to 0.66 acres, with the neighboring lot reduced accordingly. The Plan Commission had recommended approval.

**Budget Correction:** A resolution fixed a clerical error in the FY 2026-27 budget where a transfer-out figure was entered as a positive instead of a negative.

## Funding Moves

**Chamber of Commerce/Main Street Contract:** The council renewed its one-year agreement with Dixon Chamber of Commerce and Main Street, increasing funding from $325,000 to $350,000 — the first increase since the contract began three years ago. The organization indicated it does not plan to request another increase for a couple of years.

**General Fund Transfers ($4.47 million):** McCarter walked the council through a series of transfers including $1.725 million to the capital fund (for vehicles, equipment, and carryover projects from FY25), $700,000 to infrastructure for seawall repairs, $295,000 for the cemetery building payment, $20,000 for an airport grant match, and the remainder split between fire pension ($693,000) and a pension bond fund ($1,037,404).

**Municipal Band ($40,000):** The council approved a $40,000 transfer from the community development fund to cover municipal band expenses, consistent with past practice.

**Bloody Gulch Park ($1 million):** In what may be the most consequential financial action of the evening, the council transferred $1 million from the infrastructure fund to the Fargo CRIF TIF for Bloody Gulch Park improvements. The money had originally been earmarked for Project Rock but was freed up after the city secured a $4.6 million state grant for that project. Combined with a separate $2 million federal grant from Congressman LaHood, the funds will go toward phase one of the park — estimated at $4 to $5 million — which includes regional retention ponds and a pathway connecting the bridge area through to the Gateway. Langloss noted the TIF will eventually reimburse these costs through a TIF bond expected within 12 to 18 months.

## Other Business

**Zoning Referral:** A variance petition from Robinson Outdoor LLC was referred to the Zoning Board of Appeals without discussion.

**Labor and Claims:** The council approved $753,467.44 in total claims, with the largest single item being a $200,000 donation to the Historic Dixon Theater.

**Student Athletes:** Mayor Hughes devoted several minutes to recognizing Dixon students' accomplishments. The scholastic bowl team finished second to Keith Country Day (costing the mayor a bet — he wore a Rockford shirt to the meeting). In track and field, Owen LeSage won the state shot put championship. Reagan Middle School and St. Mary's students also placed at IESA state meets. Councilman Mike Venier added that Dixon's disc golf team won the state championship, with Jim Gosinski taking the individual title.

**America 250 Banners:** Councilman Chris Bishop noted that Kevin Spratt put up 250 banners downtown and credited Lucas Pauly with designing Dixon's commemorative symbol for the celebration. He urged residents to check Discover Dixon for upcoming events.

The council entered executive session to discuss probable or imminent litigation before adjourning. Councilwoman Mary Oros was absent.
