# Dixon City Council Meeting Summary — March 2, 2026

## Resident Raises Concerns About Water Billing

Jacob Payne of 1112 Steinman Street addressed the council about problems with his water service. He came home from work to find his water had been shut off without proper notice — after city offices had closed — forcing him to go without water overnight in the middle of winter.

Payne also reported that he filed a FOIA request seeking data on water disconnections from mid-February and was denied on privacy grounds. He said he wasn't asking for individual names, just aggregate numbers, arguing the public deserves to know how widespread utility disconnections are in the community.

Additionally, Payne said that while water department employee Michelle Fastler helpfully waived a $50 reconnection fee, the fee — plus roughly $5 more — still appeared on his printed bill. He asked the council to look into the accuracy of billing software to ensure waivers are actually applied before bills go out.

Councilman Veneer asked how the city would follow up. City Manager Danny Langloss said they are familiar with the situation and would address it.

## Police Chief Gives Nine-Month Progress Report

The police chief delivered a lengthy presentation on his first nine months leading the department. Key points:

- **School presence** has been significantly increased beyond just the two school resource officers. The mayor confirmed he's personally noticed officers in schools more frequently.
- **Staffing** stands at 31 officers on paper, but between a deployment, field training, two officers in the academy, and one out on injury, actual availability is well below that. One newer officer recently resigned; a replacement starts April 6 and will attend the academy in May.
- A **continuous testing** hiring process has replaced the old periodic approach, keeping a steady pipeline of applicants.
- The department launched an **officer wellness program** covering mental, physical, emotional, spiritual, and financial health. The chief joked that new officers tend to immediately buy Chevy trucks with their first paychecks.
- A **family support group** drew nearly every officer's spouse or partner to its first meeting, and an open house attracted about 150 family members.
- **Active shooter training** is being expanded to include OSF hospital, city fire, Dixon Rural, and Lee County agencies.
- A **youth mentorship program** is in development but not yet launched.
- The department is pursuing grants for new radios and a side-by-side vehicle, and is working on establishing a **mobile crisis response team** to replace the former police social worker position.

## Routine Approvals

The council unanimously approved:

- **Minutes** from the Feb. 17 regular and special sessions.
- **Claims totaling $339,967.38**, the largest single item being an $85,170 loan payment to the Illinois EPA for water infrastructure.

## Fire Department Gets New Cardiac Monitors

The council approved the purchase of two Zoll cardiac monitor/defibrillators — one for $60,338.67 paid from current capital funds, and a second for $57,303.13 on a three-year, zero-interest payment plan. The existing monitors will be repurposed: one goes to the tactical EMS team and the other will upgrade the fire engine to an ALS non-transport unit.

## Other Actions

- **Centurion health care contract:** The fire chief was authorized to sign an agreement with Centurion, the new contracted health care provider for the Illinois Department of Corrections, covering ambulance transport services for prisoners. The city attorney reviewed the contract.
- **Grant writing services:** The city renewed its agreement with CINCON Solutions for grant writing. The city manager noted CINCON has helped Dixon win over $30 million in grants over the past six to eight years, with no fee increase this year.
- **Salt purchase:** The mayor was authorized to enter the state's joint salt purchasing agreement — standard annual business.
- **Two zoning referrals** were sent to the Plan Commission: a petition from 103 East Morgan LLC for a zoning amendment, and a final plat re-subdivision of the Dixon Gateway subdivision. No discussion; these are simply referrals at this stage.
- **Tattooing age ordinance:** The council updated the city code to lower the minimum tattooing age from 21 to 18, aligning with changes the state made to the Illinois criminal code since 2007. The city attorney noted that as a non-home rule municipality, Dixon cannot impose stricter rules than state law.

## Budget Line-Item Transfers

Three budget amendments passed unanimously, all moving money between existing line items — no new spending:

- **Fire Department:** $22,000 shifted from grant match funds to cover vehicle maintenance, ladder testing, and small equipment replacement (including a well-used and finally worn-out jaws-of-life tool).
- **Police Department:** $15,000 moved to cover vehicle repairs, including a squad car involved in a fender bender. Insurance reimbursement is expected but hasn't arrived yet.
- **Street Department:** $20,000 moved from operating supplies to maintenance and repairs, driven by costly dump truck repairs.

## Electric Supply Contract

The council authorized the city manager to sign a competitive electric supply contract when bids come in March 11. The city participates in a group municipal purchasing arrangement. Last year, due to high rates, they opted for a one-year deal rather than a longer commitment. The bids are only good the day they come in, hence the need for pre-authorization.

## Nuisance Property Near New Bike Path

The city manager addressed a social media post that apparently generated significant attention over the weekend about a problem property near the new bike path around the Second Street viaduct. He said the city has issued multiple citations and referred the matter to the city attorney's office, though the legal process takes time.

## Other Notes

- Councilman Bishop congratulated the Dixon High School wrestling team, which placed fourth at state.
- The council moved into closed executive session to discuss personnel matters.
