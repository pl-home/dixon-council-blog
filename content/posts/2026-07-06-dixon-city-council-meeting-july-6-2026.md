# Dixon City Council Meeting Summary — Monday, July 7, 2026

**Present:** Mayor Glen Hughes, Councilmen Mike Venier, Chris Bishop, Dennis Considine, and Mary Oros. City Manager Danny Langloss Jr., Police Chief Ryan Bivins, and other staff also participated.

---

## Petunia Festival Recap

Rob Bates, this year's Petunia Festival president, appeared before the council to thank city departments for their support during the festival. Bates acknowledged the event had a rough start due to weather — the worst interruptions the festival has seen in years — but said crowds rebounded strongly over the weekend. He noted his 14th year of involvement with the festival.

City Manager Langloss later added that the festival will face financial strain, as the Gin Blossoms concert cancellation came with no refund. He said the council should expect to work with the festival board through the year as it tries to recover financially.

Chief Bivins reported that between July 1–5, the department handled 289 calls for service and 23 arrests, but only 26 calls and four arrests were related to the festival itself. He credited a strong police presence as a deterrent and thanked the Lee County Sheriff's Office, Sterling PD, and Illinois State Police for providing additional personnel and drone support. He also praised Deputy Chiefs Chris Scott and Aaron Simonton for months of operational planning.

The Reagan Run continued to grow, drawing roughly 1,660 participants — near a record. Runners reportedly praised the new pathway.

## Habitat for Humanity Seeks City-Owned Lot

Bob Gingress, construction committee chair for Dixon Habitat for Humanity, presented to the council. The organization is building its 34th home — currently under construction on West 9th Street — and is looking ahead to 2027. Habitat is requesting a city-owned lot at the corner of 7th Street and Madison Avenue, a parcel cleared through the city's Strong Communities demolition grant program.

City Manager Langloss endorsed the idea, and staff recommended waiving building permits and connection fees for the project. However, City Attorney Robert LeSage noted the city cannot simply donate or sell the property without going through a formal notice and bidding process required by law. The council will take this up at a future meeting.

Gingress said this year's home recipients are Phillip and Cassandra McPherson and their three children. The "Band of Brothers" volunteer crew now numbers about 22 people, turning out 12–15 workers on build days.

## Chateau Estates Gas Leak Response

Langloss reported on a significant gas leak at Chateau Estates that required the evacuation of 106 homes — the largest evacuation in Langloss's 30 years with the city. He praised the fire department's command structure, specifically Deputy Fire Chief Adam Arnold and Deputy Police Chief Chris Scott.

Reagan Middle School served as an emergency shelter. Reagan Mass Transit District sent three shuttles, and the school district provided buses. The Red Cross and Salvation Army assisted, and the Dixon Family YMCA opened its showers to displaced residents. A local laundry business offered free laundry service, and the township provided gift cards for food. Langloss called the multi-agency response "really incredible."

## E-Bikes on Pathways: Zero Tolerance

Langloss announced a zero-tolerance enforcement policy for motorized vehicles — particularly high-speed e-bikes — on city pathways. He said riders have been clocked at 30–40 mph, doing donuts on the path, and showing no regard for pedestrians. The policy: first a warning (tracked), then a ticket, then impoundment. The city plans to sort out broader e-bike regulations separately, but Langloss made clear that what he called "things that kind of look like bikes that you don't pedal" are banned from the paths effective immediately.

## Heritage Crossing Bridge Dedication

Councilman Bishop announced the official bridge dedication and pathway opening for Heritage Crossing would take place the following day (July 7) at 11 a.m. He also highlighted several America 250 improvements visible during the festival weekend, including binoculars installed under the bridge (including handicap-accessible sets), flower plantings by the Rock River Garden Club, and American flags displayed on the bridge. He credited Discover Dixon and the Park District as key partners.

## Sign Variance Approved

The council unanimously approved a height variance for Robinson Outdoor LLC to install a 39-foot electronic billboard at 1623 South Galena Avenue in the Gateway Business District — 14 feet above the standard 25-foot limit. The sign will match an existing Robinson billboard across the street. A representative, Brent DeWilde, told the council the company offers free billboard space for emergency alerts (tornado warnings, Amber Alerts, silver alerts) and community fundraisers. The Zoning Board of Appeals had recommended approval at its June 25 meeting. Advertising packages start at about $300 per month.

## Police Pension Fund Appointment

The council approved the appointment of Aaron Fox to the Board of Trustees of the Police Pension Fund for a term running through April 30, 2028. He replaces Finance Director Becky Leslie, whose term expired. Leslie will remain on the fire pension fund board.

## Routine Business

- **Minutes** from the June 15, 2026, regular session were approved.
- **Claims** totaling $501,462.98 were approved for payment.
- All votes were unanimous.

The meeting adjourned after roughly 50 minutes.
