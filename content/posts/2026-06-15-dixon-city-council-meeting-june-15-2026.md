# Dixon City Council Meeting Summary
## Regular Meeting — Monday, June 15, 2026

**Present:** Mayor Glen Hughes, Councilman Mary Oros, Councilman Dennis Considine. **Absent:** Councilman Chris Bishop, Councilman Mike Venier (no quorum issues; business proceeded with three voting members).

---

### Claims and Finances

The council approved $1,834,316.37 in total labor and outside claims. Two large items of note: a roll-off truck purchase at $124,556 (previously budgeted) and a $1,221,472 payment for ongoing wastewater treatment plant improvements. Year-to-date financials through May 2026 were accepted without discussion.

### Electrical Aggregation Renewed — Savings Returning for Residents

The council approved renewal of the city's municipal electrical aggregation program. Adam Hoover of NEMAC, the city's long-time aggregation partner, presented the details. For the past couple of years, the program had operated as a price-match arrangement offering no savings to residents but generating $20,000 annually for the city. That program expires in September.

Good news for ratepayers: NEMAC secured a one-year rate of 9.55 cents per kilowatt-hour through MC Squared, compared to ComEd's projected rate of roughly 9.96 cents — an estimated savings of about $50 per household over the year. Hoover cautioned that ComEd's rate fluctuates monthly, so in any given month ComEd might be cheaper, but over the full year the aggregation rate should deliver net savings. Residents can opt out at any time with no penalty. Hoover also noted the city is saving approximately $105,000 annually on its own municipal electric accounts through a separate group bid.

### Water and Sewer Rate Adjustments

Two ordinances restructured how this year's already-approved utility rate increase is applied. The key point: **no new rate increase is being added.** The city's long-term plan caps annual increases at 2.5%. Finance Director Becky Leslie (referenced as "Ms. McCarter" in discussion, likely a transcription error) explained that the wastewater fund is in better financial shape than the water fund, so this year's increase will be directed entirely to the water system. Most households will see an increase of roughly 2.3 to 2.5 percent. The old "adjusted rate increase" (ARI) from the 2020 rate study is being eliminated, and the rate tables are being simplified. Staff will reassess both funds next year.

### Vehicle Purchase and Disposal

The council approved disposal of a 2008 Ford F-250 with 123,000 miles from the water department — described as constantly breaking down with a deteriorating body. Its replacement, a 2026 GMC 2500 HD, was purchased from Ken Nelson Auto Plaza in Dixon for $55,513 (after a $3,500 trade-in), coming in $4,800 under budget. Mayor Hughes noted his appreciation for buying local.

### Library Board Appointments

Mayor Hughes thanked Rachel Kokar (9 years) and Terry Dunphy (15 years) for their service on the Dixon Public Library Board of Trustees, both of whom are stepping down.

Three appointments were approved:
- **Marcella Kitson** — reappointed for a three-year term. She has served 10 years.
- **Melinda Mills** — new appointment. A regular library user, community volunteer, and mother of a special needs child.
- **Leah Stock** — new appointment. A recent Dixon High School graduate who will attend Sauk Valley Community College and plans to remain in the Dixon community. She was present in the audience. Mayor Hughes said he wanted to see more youth involvement on city boards.

The library board has nine members with staggered terms; three seats were up this cycle.

### Zoning Board Reappointment

Cynthia Adcock was reappointed to the Zoning Board of Appeals for a five-year term. She has served three years. Mayor Hughes noted the board meets irregularly, so the longer term provides stability.

### YMCA Tax Agreement

The council approved a TIF redevelopment agreement with the Dixon Family YMCA for $99,975.22. City Manager Danny Langloss explained this stems from a paperwork miscommunication during the property's transfer from Dixon Land Group to the YMCA. Because the nonprofit status wasn't properly recorded, a property tax bill was generated. The city will pay the county directly on the YMCA's behalf, then recoup the money through TIF reimbursement. Langloss noted that had the YMCA been a tax-paying entity, the city would have entered into a substantial TIF agreement with them from the start — but since nonprofits don't pay property tax, no such agreement existed. The issue is expected to be resolved going forward.

### EMS Billing Outsourced

Fire Chief Ryan Buskohl requested — and the council approved — an agreement with Paramedic Billing Services Inc., an Illinois company specializing in emergency medical services billing. The fire department has handled EMS billing internally up to now, but increasing transport volume and the complexity of insurance, Medicare, and Medicaid billing prompted the move. Buskohl called it "best practice going forward."

### Mayor's Remarks

Mayor Hughes used his comments to address a recurring complaint: **dog owners not cleaning up after their pets** on the city's newer amenities, specifically the riverfront path out to 7th Street and the bridge and pathway into Page Park. He acknowledged it's likely a small percentage of dog owners but said he has received multiple complaints and urged residents to be responsible.

### Council Comments

Councilman Considine praised the Midwest Leadership Summit for drawing visitors downtown and generating positive feedback, and thanked city staff and sponsors. He also highlighted the work of Beautify Dixon, Blooming on the Rock, and the Garden Club, noting that many volunteers are aging and could use fresh help. He encouraged residents to get involved in monthly community cleanup days.

The meeting adjourned with no executive session and no further business. It was a notably short meeting with no work session and only three council members present.

Councilman Dennis Considine's tenure on the council extends back more than a decade. He appears in the roll call of the April 1, 2013 regular meeting, when he served as a commissioner under Mayor Burke. He continued serving through the transition to the council-manager form, appearing at the December 3, 2018 meeting under Mayor Arellano and again at the June 7, 2021 meeting. His presence at the current June 15, 2026 meeting, now under Mayor Glen Hughes, marks at least his third mayoral administration as a sitting member.

Councilman Mary Oros is recorded as present at the June 7, 2021 regular meeting under Mayor Arellano, indicating she has served on the council for at least five years heading into the current meeting.

Councilman Mike Venier, absent from the current meeting, was present at the December 3, 2018 meeting and was noted as absent from the June 7, 2021 meeting as well. His pattern of occasional absences appears in earlier records, though the council has consistently maintained a quorum regardless.

Councilman Chris Bishop, also absent from the current meeting, was present at the June 7, 2021 meeting.

The archived excerpts do not contain material related to specific agenda items from the current meeting, so no topical historical connections can be drawn from these records. The excerpts do, however, show a notable shift in meeting structure over time: meetings under Mayor Burke in 2010 and 2013 were held at 6:30 p.m. with members referred to as commissioners, while meetings from 2018 onward under Mayor Arellano began at 5:30 p.m. with members referred to as councilmen, reflecting Dixon's change in governmental form. The current meeting continues that later format.
