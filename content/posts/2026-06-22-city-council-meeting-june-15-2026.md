# Dixon City Council Meeting Summary — June 15, 2026

**A brief, business-heavy meeting with only three council members present.**

The Dixon City Council met Monday evening with a reduced roster: Mayor Glen Hughes, Councilman Mary Oros, and Councilman Dennis Considine were present. Councilmen Chris Bishop and Mike Venier were absent.

## Bills and Finances

The council approved $1,834,316.37 in total labor and outside claims. Two big-ticket items were flagged: a roll-off truck purchase at $124,556 (previously budgeted) and a $1,221,472 payment toward the ongoing wastewater treatment plant improvements. Year-to-date financials through May 2026 were accepted without discussion.

## Electric Aggregation Renewed — Savings Expected This Time

Adam Hoover of Nemec, the city's energy aggregation partner, presented what he called the best rate his firm has found for any of its 45 client communities. The city's residential aggregation program, which had shifted to a break-even "price match" arrangement in recent years, can now offer actual savings again.

MC Squared, the current supplier, offered a one-year rate of 9.55 cents per kilowatt-hour, compared to ComEd's projected rate of roughly 10 cents. Hoover estimated about $50 in annual savings per household and recommended the one-year option. He cautioned that ComEd's rate fluctuates monthly, so there may be a month or two where ComEd dips below the aggregation rate, but guaranteed overall savings across the year. He also noted the city saves approximately $105,000 annually through a separate group bid for municipal electric accounts.

Residents can opt in or out of the program at any time with no penalty. The council approved the renewal unanimously.

## Water and Sewer Rates Restructured — No New Increase

Finance Director Becky Leslie explained two ordinances that restructure how this year's already-approved utility rate increase is applied. The key points:

- **No additional increase** beyond what the council previously approved under a long-term plan capping annual hikes at 2.5%.
- The wastewater fund is in stronger financial shape than the water fund, so this year's approved increase is being directed entirely to the water system.
- Most households will see increases of roughly 2.3 to 2.5 percent — below the current inflation rate.
- The old "adjusted rate increase" formula from the 2020 rate study is being eliminated in favor of a simpler structure.

Staff and council will reassess both funds next year. Both ordinances passed unanimously.

## Vehicle Swap

The council approved disposing of a 2008 Ford F-250 with 123,000 miles from the Water Department — described as constantly breaking down with a deteriorating body. Its replacement, a 2026 GMC 2500 HD, was purchased from Ken Nelson Auto Plaza in Dixon for $55,513 (including a $3,500 trade-in credit), coming in about $4,800 under budget. Mayor Hughes noted his appreciation for keeping the purchase local.

## YMCA Property Tax Fix

City Manager Danny Langloss explained a $99,975.22 TIF redevelopment agreement with the Dixon Family YMCA to resolve a paperwork mix-up. As a nonprofit, the YMCA should have been exempt from property taxes on its site, but a miscommunication between the YMCA and the developer, Dixon Land Group, meant the proper exemption paperwork was never filed. The county also failed to catch the issue. The city will pay the county directly on the YMCA's behalf and recoup the money through TIF reimbursement. Langloss noted that had the YMCA been a taxpaying entity, the city would have entered into a much larger TIF agreement with them from the start.

## EMS Billing Outsourced

Fire Chief Ryan Buskohl requested approval to contract with Paramedic Billing Services Inc., an Illinois company specializing in EMS transport billing. The department has handled billing internally but said increasing transport volume and the complexity of insurance, Medicare, and Medicaid billing make outsourcing the better path forward. Approved unanimously.

## Library Board Appointments

Mayor Hughes thanked departing Library Board members Rachel Cocar (9 years of service) and Terry Dunphy (15 years). Three appointments were approved:

- **Marcella Kitson** — reappointed after 10 years of service.
- **Melinda Mills** — new appointee; a regular library user and community volunteer with a special needs child.
- **Leah Stock** — new appointee and recent Dixon High School graduate headed to Sauk Valley Community College. Hughes said he wanted to bring younger voices to the board.

The Library Board has nine members with three seats rotating each year.

## Other Appointments

**Cynthia Adcock** was reappointed to the Zoning Board of Appeals for a five-year term. Hughes noted the board meets irregularly, so the longer term provides stability.

## Stray Comments

Mayor Hughes issued a pointed reminder to dog owners using the city's new riverfront path to 7th Street and the bridge and pathway into Page Park: clean up after your pets. He said he's received multiple complaints.

Councilman Oros praised the recent Midwest Leadership Summit for drawing visitors downtown and commended volunteer groups — Beautify Dixon, Bloomin' on the Rock, and the Garden Club — for their work maintaining the city's appearance. She encouraged residents to volunteer, noting many of the current volunteers are aging and could use reinforcements.

The meeting adjourned quickly, with no public comment and no executive session.

**Electric Aggregation**

Dixon's electric aggregation program has a well-documented history stretching back more than a decade. The concept was first introduced to the council on October 17, 2011, when Mike Mudge presented an overview of municipal aggregation and cited savings achieved in nearby communities such as Fulton. Discussion continued on November 19, 2012, when Marni Henert of Rock River Energy Services presented the possibility of placing a municipal aggregation question on the April ballot. The matter moved forward with an ordinance placed on file on April 15, 2013, and was formally adopted on May 6, 2013, when the council passed Ordinance #2909 authorizing aggregation of electrical load and adopting a plan of operation and governance. The June 15, 2026 renewal with MC Squared, presented by Adam Hoover of Nemec, represents a continuation of this long-running program. The council's discussion noted that the program had shifted to a break-even "price match" arrangement in recent years, making the newly secured 9.55-cent rate and its projected household savings a notable development for a program now in its thirteenth year.

**Water and Sewer Rates**

The current restructuring of water and sewer rates builds on a series of prior rate actions. Water and sewer rates were among the topics discussed at a special council meeting on March 14, 2022, alongside ARPA funds, budget changes, and other fiscal matters. The council subsequently passed Ordinance #3309 on June 6, 2022, amending the city code sections governing both water rates and sewer rates. A further sewer rate amendment, Ordinance #3316, followed on July 18, 2022. The two ordinances approved on June 15, 2026, which redirect the approved annual increase entirely to the water fund and eliminate the "adjusted rate increase" formula from the 2020 rate study, represent the latest adjustment in this ongoing rate-setting process. Finance Director Leslie's reference to a long-term plan capping annual hikes at 2.5% appears to be rooted in the framework established during this earlier period.

**EMS Billing**

The decision to outsource EMS billing to Paramedic Billing Services Inc. follows a recent internal transition. On February 2, 2026, Fire Chief Buskohl reported to the council that the department had just started using a new EMS billing system. The move to a fully outsourced billing contractor roughly four months later suggests the internal system did not resolve the volume and complexity challenges the chief cited at the June 15 meeting.

**Library Board Appointments**

Library Board appointments have appeared regularly in council records over many years, consistent with the three-seats-per-year rotation described at this meeting. Prior appointment actions are documented on June 16, 2008 (reappointments of Kim Pettygrove, Cynthia Zuend, David Badger, and Alice Collins), June 21, 2010 (reappointment of Robert Thomas), and January 21, 2014 (appointment of Carol Chandler and reappointment of Roberta Vanderlin). The reappointment of Marcella Kitson and the seating of two new members, Melinda Mills and Leah Stock, continue this regular cycle.

**YMCA Property Tax Fix**

The archived excerpts include several YMCA-related items, but none directly address the property tax exemption paperwork issue or the TIF redevelopment agreement approved at this meeting. Prior YMCA mentions involve a temporary road closure for the Shamrock Shuffle on December 3, 2018, and a related liquor code amendment on December 17, 2018, as well as a 2011 Plan Commission discussion about property near the YMCA. The $99,975.22 TIF agreement to resolve the YMCA's missed exemption filing appears to be a new matter before the council.
